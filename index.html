Explore the Age of Dinosaurs

The Tyrannosauridae family tree shows how large meat-eating dinosaurs evolved from earlier relatives into more advanced predators. Over time, species like Albertosaurus and Daspletosaurus developed stronger jaws, bigger bodies, and better hunting abilities. At the end of this evolutionary line, Tyrannosaurus rex became one of the largest and most powerful members of the family.

The Pterodactyl family tree shows how flying reptiles evolved into many different species over time. Early forms like Pterodactylus developed wings and lightweight bodies for flight, while later species such as Pteranodon and Tapejara became larger and more specialized. This evolution led to a wide variety of pterosaurs adapted for different ways of flying and feeding.
